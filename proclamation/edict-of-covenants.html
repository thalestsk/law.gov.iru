Edict of Covenants

A sovereign expression of authority and interpretation of the Founding Proclamation on matters relating to the covenants arising from the relationship between the State and the individual.
Reference No: EDICT-III Year of Proclamation: 1943

His Imperial Majesty Emperor Thales XIII, Sovereign of the State of Common Good, with the authority vested by the Proclamation, hereby proclaims:

(i) the unalienable right of the State to assert its authority over all persons and matters within the Domain, and in so doing declares the ordering of rights and obligations between the State and those subject to it;

(ii) obedience to the laws of the State, compliance with its lawful directions, and the bearing of such duties as are necessary for the preservation and advancement of the Domain. The State asserts the right to act, to regulate, and to determine matters of governance as may be deemed necessary and expedient;

(iii) that the State grants to all persons within the Domain the protection of the law, who shall not be deprived of life, liberty, or possession save by lawful authority. The State grants that justice shall be applied without arbitrariness, and that all causes shall be heard and determined in accordance with law;

(iv) that, for the preservation of order and the quiet conduct of life within the Domain, there shall be maintained a sphere of private life, within which persons may conduct their affairs, relations, and dispositions without undue intrusion and undue influence of any entity outside of State, save where the State deems it necessary for the sanctity of the Domain;

(v) that the State alone determines the conditions under which persons may remain within or depart from the Domain;

(vi) that any and all rights granted or delegated by the State are functional and contingent upon the maintenance of the Domain and the ultimate authority of the Sovereign, and not inherent or absolute;

(vii) that in the resolution of conflicting covenants, obligations and recognitions, private or public, that understanding shall prevail which affirms the primacy, unity, and continuity of the State.

and here so ordained, the covenant within the Domain, that authority may remain whole, order maintained, and all within it sustained in unity under the Sovereign, in whom all power resides undivided.
