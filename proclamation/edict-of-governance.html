Edict of Governance

A sovereign expression of authority and interpretation of the Founding Proclamation on matters relating to governance and administration.
Reference No: EDICT-II Year of Proclamation: 2012

His Imperial Majesty Emperor Thales XIII, Sovereign of the State of Common Good, with the authority vested by the Proclamation, hereby proclaims:

(i) the constituting of His Imperial Majesty's Government ("the Government") as the collective body for the ordering, direction, and administration of the affairs of the Domain. The Government shall stand in unity with the Sovereign, and shall act as the instrument through which the will and direction of the Sovereign are made effective in the governance of the State.

(ii) the composition of the Government is determined at the sole discretion of the Sovereign, and the persons there of appointed and ordered shall:

(a) hold office at the pleasure of the Sovereign,

(b) exercise such functions as are entrusted to them,

(c) support the lawful and orderly discharge of functions within the Government,

(d) and act in unity for the execution of the Sovereign’s direction and the preservation of the State.

(iii) the establishment of the Estates-General as the body through which counsel, consultation, and the articulation of matters arising within the Domain are received, considered, and given form in law, and as the designated forum for:

(a) the public deliberation upon matters concerning the ordering of the State,

(b) the receiving and considering of input from the Domain as may be prudent,

(c) the enactment of written law in the form of Acts of Estates for the express structuring of governance and the establishment of offices, departments, and functions of the State,

(d) and the codification of such principles as shall guide the exercise of executive authority as is reflective of the Sovereign's intent.

(iv) that the Estates-General shall be received as a unified body of counsel and ordinance, notwithstanding the diversity of its composition, and shall act in such manner as gives coherent form to the governance of the State under the Sovereign.

(v) that the State asserts the right to require information, counsel, and input from persons and bodies within the Domain as may be necessary for the proper ordering of governance. Such input shall be received and considered through such means as the State directs, and shall serve the function of informing the deliberations of the Estates-General and the actions of Government. No claim shall lie to compel the State to act upon such input, which shall be held as contributory to, but not determinative of, the governance of the Domain.

(vi) that the Government, acting under the direction of the Sovereign and within the framework of the Acts of Estates, shall administer the affairs of the Domain and give effect to the ordinances of the State. To this end, the Government may from time to time and as is necessary:

(a) issue regulations for necessary clarification and execution of Acts,

(b) organise and direct the departments and officers of the State,

(c) and act with such discretion as is required for the effective governance of the Domain.

(vii) that no function of governance shall be exercised in a manner that frustrates or opposes the other, but all shall be directed toward the coherent and effective administration of the Domain under the Sovereign.

(viii) the standard of accountability on the exercise of executive authority shall be held by reference to, in part or in whole, for the avoidance of deviation, excess, or insufficiency, and likewise for the correction thereafter:

(a) the direction of the Sovereign,

(b) the principles and structure established by the Acts of the Estates,

(c) and the preservation of the State.

And here so ordained the governance of the Domain, that authority may be directed, law given form, and administration carried out in unity and cooperation under the Sovereign, in whom all power resides undivided.
