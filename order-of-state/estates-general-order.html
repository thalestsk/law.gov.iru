Estates-General Order 2024

An Order of State for the structuring of authority and responsibility within the Estates-General.
Reference No: OS.2024/02 Enacted: 1 January 2024

By Order of His Imperial Majesty Emperor Thales XIII, Sovereign of the State of Common Good

1. Short title, etc.

This Order of State is the Estates-General Order 2024 as promulgated on 1 January 2024.

2. The Estates

The Estates-General comprises five (5) Estates in ascending order of precedence:

(a) the Congress of the Prefectures (the "Congress");

(b) the Chamber of Distinction (the "Chamber");

(c) the Conference of Governors (the "Conference");

(d) the Standing Committee of the Estates-General (the "Standing Committee");

(e) the Assembly of Senators of State (the "Senate");

3. Presiding Officer

(1) Except for the Standing Committee, each Estate shall elect from amongst its members a Presiding Officer to preside over its proceedings.

(2) The term of office for a Presiding Officer shall be indefinite and expires upon the election of a new Presiding Officer.

(3) A Presiding Officer may nominate no more than three (3) Deputy Presiding Officers, subject to the confirmation within the Estate. The first Deputy Presiding Officer to be nominated and confirmed shall be the Acting Presiding Officer in the absence or incapacity of the Presiding Officer.

4. The Congress

(1) The members of the Congress (the "Congressmen") shall be the elected representative of each Prefecture. The electoral process for Congressmen shall be established by written law.

(2) The Congress is the official forum for the receipt, discussion and debate of petitions and proposals from the State and its subjects.

(3) The Congress may adopt resolutions on petitions and proposals by a simple majority vote. Resolutions shall be the official record of the discussion and debate conducted by the Congress, and summarised with its findings, conclusions and recommendations.

(4) Where a governing board for a statutory department of the Government allocates a position on such a board to a member of the Estates-General, the Congress may nominate from within the Congress or the Chamber to serve on such a board by a simple majority vote.

5. The Chamber

(1) The members of the Chamber (the "Fellows") shall be elected from amongst the body of persons who have been accorded peerages by the Sovereign.

(2) The number of Fellows shall be the closest odd number that is no more than two-thirds of the number of Congressmen.

(3) The Chamber is the forum for the ensuring of the completeness and comprehensiveness of resolutions adopted by the Congress.

(4) The Chamber shall review all resolutions adopted by Congress and adopt its own addendums thereto by a simple majority vote. Addendums shall be the official record of the review conducted by the Chamber, and summarised with its findings, conclusions and recommendations.

(5) The Chamber may object to a nomination to a governing board by the Congress insofar as the nomination relates to a Fellow by a simple majority vote, following which the Congress shall nominate another Congressman or Fellow.

6. The Conference

(1) The members of the Conference (the "Governors") shall be the Intendants-General and the Ministers appointed by the First Ministers of each Canton as the delegate to the Conference.

(2) The Conference shall:

(a) review the resolution adopted by the Congress together with the addendum adopted by the Chamber;

(b) upon review of any resolution and addendum, remit the same to the Congress for reconsideration, following which Congress shall adopt a new resolution that may affirm or amend its previous resolution, which shall then be sent to the Chamber for its adoption of an addendum thereto; or transmit the same to the Senate for its deliberation;

(c) upon a nomination by the Congress not objected to by the Chamber, conduct a hearing on the nomination;

(d) upon a nomination by a body for an appointment or office where the power of confirmation has been conferred upon the Estates-General, conduct a hearing on the nomination;

(e) upon the conclusion of a hearing, confirm the nomination by a simple majority vote.

(3) The Conference may:

(a) originate a petition for the consideration of the Congress;

(b) at the request of a Governor, seconded by at least eight (8) other Governors, scrutinise a Statute of Expenditure enacted by the Parliament of a Canton, in which the Governors of that Canton may not request or second such a request; and

(c) upon scrutiny of a Statute of Expenditure, recommend to the Sovereign to veto such a Statute of Expenditure by a two-thirds majority vote of the Conference, where the exercise of the veto is at the discretion of the Sovereign for which the Sovereign may direct either or both the Parliament and the Conference to reconsider their respective positions on the Statute of Expenditure prior to the Sovereign's exercise of the veto.

7. The Standing Committee

(1) The members of the Standing Committee (the "Standing Committee Members") shall be:

(a) the Presiding Officers;

(b) the Deputy Presiding Officers;

(c) the four (4) members elected by each Estate to serve on the Standing Committee, in addition to the Presiding Officers and the Deputy Presiding Officers.

(2) The Chairman of the Standing Committee shall:

(a) preside over the proceedings of the Standing Committee; and

(b) be elected from within the Standing Committee by the Standing Committee Members.

(3) The Standing Committee shall:

(a) establish and prescribe additional procedures for the orderly conduct of business for the Estates of the Estates-General in accordance with this Order;

(b) determine the remuneration, benefits and privileges of the members of the Estates-General;

(c) establish a code of conduct and the penalties for violations thereof for all members of the Estates-General;

(d) conduct investigative hearings into allegations of violations of the code of conduct and determine the imposition of penalties in accordance with the code of conduct; and

(e) establish and oversee the organisations and administrative apparatus required for the effective conduct of business in the Estates-General.

(4) The Chairman of the Standing Committee is concurrently the Honourary President of the Estates-General.

8. The Senate

(1) The members of the Senate (the "Senators") shall be appointed by the Sovereign and shall serve at his pleasure.

(2) In the exercise of legislative authority, the Senate shall take a holistic approach in the consideration of all matters brought before it, including but not limited to the resolutions and addendums transmitted to it by the Conference, extraneous material publicly available, the broader public policy considerations and implications for the State, and other considerations deemed appropriate by the Senate, such that the decision is consistent with the Sovereign's direction with respect to the perpetuation of the Common Good.

(3) The Senate is not bound by a vote, but may do so to express unanimity and consensus. Any decision of the Senate shall be deemed to have been a unanimous decision of the Senate.

(4) An Act of Estates shall be enacted by a decision of the Senate.

(5) The Senate, at the direction of the Sovereign, shall enact Acts of Estates required for the proper functioning of the institutions of the State in order to preserve and further the Common Good, without having been originated by petition from the Congress or discussed in any Estate.

\

(6) Where the Senate is directed by the Sovereign to investigate the suitability of candidates and incumbent officeholders for the office of Intendant-General, the Senate shall do so expeditiously and advise the Sovereign of its non-binding recommendation for appointment or removal.

9. Term of Office

(1) Where a member of an Estate is elected, the term of office shall be four (4) years.

(2) There are no term limits unless otherwise provided for in written law.

(3) The minimum eligibility to serve in an Estate shall be citizenship and capacity. Additional eligibility criteria may be established by written law.

10. Nature of Business

The conduct of business in the Estates-General shall be public, except for the conduct of business in the Senate which shall remain private unless otherwise directed by the Sovereign to be made public.

11. Protonotary of the Estates

(1) The Protonatary of the Estates (the "Protonotary") is the chief administrative officer of the Estates-General.

(2) The Protonotary shall be nominated by the Government and appointed by the Standing Committee. The Protonotary shall report directly to the Standing Committee on the administrative affairs of the Estates-General.

(3) For each Estate of the Estates-General, the Protonotary shall appoint a Deputy Protonotary to assist with his administrative duties pertaining to that Estate. The Protonotary and Deputy Protonotaries shall organise their staff accordingly.

(4) The general function of the Protonotary shall be to ensure the proper functioning of the Estates-General and maintenance of the records of the Estates-General, and other duties that the Standing Committee may assign to the Protonotary.

(5) Notwithstanding its service to the Estates-General, the Protonotary, Deputy Protonotaries and their staff shall be considered as employees of the Government and derive their remuneration, privileges and authority accordingly.

12. Sessions of the Estates-General

Each Estate shall convene according to it legislative schedule set forth by the Standing Committee and its respective Presiding Officers, or when directed by the Sovereign to do so.

13. Committees

Each Estate may form subordinate committees and appoint its members thereto. A committee may be standing or select, and shall be for the purpose of collecting, studying and reporting on matters defined within the specific terms of reference of the committee.
