Armed Forces Order 2017

An Order of State for the establishment of the Imperialean National Armed Forces.
Reference No: OS.2017/01 Enacted: 1 January 2017

By Order of His Imperial Majesty Emperor Thales XIII, Sovereign of the State of Common Good

1. Short title, etc.

This Order of State is the Armed Forces Order 2017 as promulgated on 1 January 2017.

2. Imperialean National Armed Forces

(1) The Imperialean National Armed Forces (the "Armed Forces") is hereby established and constituted as the military component of the State.

(2) The Sovereign is the commander-in-chief of the Armed Forces.

3. Armed Forces Code

The Armed Forces Code is the statutory law that governs the operations, structure, and administration of the Armed Forces.

4. Service Members

(1) A service member is any person employed in the service of the Armed Forces.

(2) A service member, during his time in service and any other period in time specified under written law, shall be exempt from any penal provision outside of the Armed Forces Code.

(3) A service member, upon entering into service shall be bound by the non-penal provisions of the Armed Forces Code as may be amended from time to time, until otherwise discharged by any written law.

5. Chief of Armed Forces

(1) The Chief of Armed Forces is the highest-ranking service member in the Armed Forces and is appointed by the Sovereign.

(2) The Chief of Armed Forces shall be the executor of the Sovereign's command over the Armed Forces and shall advise the Sovereign where appropriate.

(3) The Chief of Armed Forces may amend the Armed Forces Code from time to time at his discretion.

(4) The Chief of Armed Forces may delegate authority to other service members as necessary.

(5) The Chief of Armed Forces shall organise the Armed Forces as is necessary for the effective defence of the State, based on the resources available at his disposal.

6. Estates-General

The Estates-General may not directly provide for matters of national defence, and where a provision on a matter of principle or general governance may be applicable, such a provision shall not be constructed or construed to impede or encumber the lawful execution of the Sovereign's command over the Armed Forces.
