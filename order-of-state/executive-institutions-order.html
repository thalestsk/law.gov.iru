Executive Institutions Order 2026

An Order of State for the hierarchy, structure and governance of executive institutions.
Reference No: OS.2026/02 Enacted: 1 January 2026

By Order of His Imperial Majesty Emperor Thales XIII, Sovereign of the State of Common Good

1. Short title, etc.

This Order of State is the Executive Institutions Order 2026 as promulgated on 1 January 2026.

2. Executive Institution

(1) This Order is applicable to all executive institutions deriving an extent of their authority from Acts of Estates.

(2) An executive institution is defined as a department subordinated under the Government that exercises administrative, regulatory or operational functions on behalf of the Sovereign, that does not derive its powers and functions directly from an Order of State.

(3) An Act of Estate establishing an executive institution shall constitute such institution as a:

(a) Crown Authority,

(b) Crown Commission,

(c) Statutory Institution, or

(d) Statutory Corporation.

(4) Any executive institution previously helmed by an Officer-General of the Crown prior to 1 January 2026 shall be constituted or reconstituted as a Crown Authority, until otherwise directed by the Sovereign.

3. General Secretariat of the Crown

The General Secretariat of the Crown (the "Secretariat") shall be the central administrative body responsible for coordinating the operations of all executive institutions and ensuring the effective communication and implementation of the Sovereign's direction within the Government.

4. Departments of State

The Secretariat shall be composed of the following Departments of State:

(a) the Department of State for the Crown,

(b) the Department of State for the Economy,

(c) the Department of State for the Enlightenment,

(d) the Department of State for the Communities, and

(e) the Department of State for the Lands.

5. Department of State for the Crown

The leadership of the Department of State for the Crown shall comprise:

(a) the Secretary of State for the Crown,

(b) the Undersecretary for Policy,

(c) the Undersecretary for Administration,

(d) the Undersecretary for Legislation, and

(e) the Undersecretary for Coordination.

6. Department of State for the Economy

The leadership of the Department of State for the Economy shall comprise:

(a) the Secretary of State for the Economy,

(b) the Undersecretary for Commerce,

(c) the Undersecretary for Industry,

(d) the Undersecretary for Resources, and

(e) the Undersecretary for Digitalisation.

7. Department of State for the Enlightenment

The leadership of the Department of State for the Enlightenment shall comprise:

(a) the Secretary of State for the Enlightenment,

(b) the Undersecretary for Education,

(c) the Undersecretary for Identity, and

(d) the Undersecretary for Information.

8. Department of State for the Communities

The leadership of the Department of State for the Communities shall comprise:

(a) the Secretary of State for the Communities,

(b) the Undersecretary for Health,

(c) the Undersecretary for Employment,

(d) the Undersecretary for Families.

9. Department of State for the Lands

The leadership of the Department of State for the Lands shall comprise:

(a) the Secretary of State for the Lands,

(b) the Undersecretary for Environment,

(c) the Undersecretary for Urbanisation, and

(d) the Undersecretary for Infrastructure.

10. Appointment to Office

(1) A Secretary of State shall be appointed by the Sovereign and shall serve at the pleasure of the Sovereign.

(2) An Undersecretary shall be appointed by the Sovereign and shall serve at the pleasure of the Sovereign.

(3) A Secretary of State may appoint Deputy Undersecretaries and Assistant Undersecretaries to assist an Undersecretary in his duties.

(4) A Deputy Undersecretary or an Assistant Undersecretary may further appoint other officials to assist them in their duties, with the concurrence of the Undersecretary.

11. Mission of the Departments of State

The portfolio prescribed to each Secretary and Undersecretary shall not be taken to limit the scope of their responsibility in respect of their missions and contributions towards the overall governance of the State, but shall be construed as a joint responsibility to achieve policy synergies as a holistic approach in pursuit of the Common Good in concert with and in service of the Sovereign.

12. Scope of Secretary of State

(1) A Secretary of State shall exercise their functions in respect of their designated portfolio with the primary object of ensuring the alignment of the institutional framework within that portfolio to the governance and policy objectives of the Government as directed by the Sovereign.

(2) The responsibility of a Secretary of State shall be to ensure that the institutions and instruments of their portfolio do not obstruct, impede, or act inconsistently with the policy objectives of the Government, and where such institutions and instruments are capable of contributing to the advancement of those objectives, to so direct and coordinate their deployment accordingly.

(3) Without prejudice to the character, mandate, or authority of any executive institution within their portfolio, a Secretary of State shall identify and pursue such coordination with other portfolios as is necessary to ensure that the resources, capacities, and regulatory authority of their portfolio are available in the service of the Government's broader objectives, whether or not those objectives originate within their designated portfolio.

(4) The Secretary of State bears sole and direct responsibility to the Sovereign for the coherent discharge of his coordination functions and the overall alignment of their department in achieving the stated policy objectives.

13. Crown Authority

(1) A Crown Authority is an executive institution that is established to support the functions of an Officer-General of the Crown (an “Officer-General”), where executive authority is formally vested in its Officer-General, who shall be its chief executive.

(2) The Estates-General may provide for the formalisation of powers and procedures of an Officer-General where the Sovereign has not already provided for.

(3) An Officer-General shall be appointed by the Sovereign and shall serve at the pleasure of the Sovereign.

(4) The positions listed in the First Schedule shall be the Officers-General of the Crown and shall have a Crown Authority constituted for the exercise of their functions.

14. SECTION

(1) A Crown Commission is an executive institution commissioned by the Estates-General on behalf of the Sovereign on a specific matter of policy and shall be of investigatory or regulatory character.

(2) A Crown Commission shall be led by no fewer than five (5) Commission Members appointed by the Estates-General, who shall have sufficient relevant experience in the scope of the Crown Commission or in public service.

(3) The Commission Members shall elect from amongst themselves a Commissioner, primus inter pares, as its chairperson and chief executive over the Commission Secretariat.

(4) The Commission Secretariat shall be the administrative body of a Crown Commission through which the powers and authority vested in the Crown Commission shall be exercised.

(5) The Crown Commission shall make determinations in respect of its powers and functions as a collective, where any vote in respect of a determination amongst the Commission Members shall be by two-thirds majority.

(6) Unless otherwise expressly provided for by the Estates-General, a Crown Commission may not issue Regulations.

15. Statutory Institution

(1) A Statutory Institution is an executive institution constituted in an Act of Estates and comprises its Board of Directors and its Managing Director.

(2) The Board of Directors of a Statutory Institution shall comprise Directors, of which:

(a) five (5) shall be appointed by the Secretary of State from outside of the Government, in concurrence with the advice of the Undersecretary whose scope coincides with the powers and function of a Statutory Institution,

(b) two (2) shall be appointed by the Estates-General,

(c) one (1) shall be an Assistant Undersecretary assigned by the Undersecretary,

(d) one (1) shall be a chief executive of any other executive institution appointed by the Sovereign,

(e) one (1) shall be a Prefect-General appointed by the Sovereign, and

(f) one (1) shall be the Managing Director of the Statutory Institution.

(3) The Chairman of the Board of Directors of a Statutory Institution shall be appointed from the Directors by the Secretary of State, who shall preside over the meetings of the Board of Directors.

(4) The Managing Director shall be the chief executive of a Statutory Institution and shall report directly to its Board of Directors.

(5) The Board of Directors shall, on a quarterly basis and in the absence of any other period specified by its authorising Act of Estates:

(a) provide oversight consistent with the Act of Estates;

(b) supervise the performance of the Statutory Institution;

(c) review the exercise of powers by the Managing Director;

(d) ensure proper governance, accountability, and internal controls;

(e) approve fiscal matters, including requests for budgets and major expenditures;

(f) approve policies and matters necessary for corporate governance;

(g) advise the Secretary of State on the appointment and removal of the Managing Director; and

(h) perform such functions as are necessary for the proper governance of the Statutory Institution.

(6) The Managing Director shall in respect of his Statutory Institution:

(a) be responsible for the day-to-day management;

(b) implement the directions of the Board of Directors and the Sovereign;

(c) exercise executive authority over all officers and operations;

(d) ensure compliance with the Act of Estates and applicable law; and

(e) perform such functions as are necessary for effective administration.

(7) In respect of Regulations issued under the Statutory Institution, the Managing Director shall seek the approval of the Board of Directors in the issuance of Regulations and may consult with the Board of Directors on the enforcement of Regulations.

(8) A Statutory Institution may hold assets to further the policy objectives sought by itself, but the interest in such holdings shall be of a secondary nature.

16. Statutory Corporation

(1) A Statutory Corporation is an executive institution constituted in an Act of Estate for the purpose of generating revenue through commercial activities that are of strategic significance or underserved by the private sector and comprises its Board of Trustees and its Chief Executive.

(2) The Board of Trustees of a Statutory Corporation shall comprise Trustees, of which:

(a) five (5) shall be appointed by the Secretary of State from outside of the Government, in concurrence with the advice of the Undersecretary whose scope coincides with the powers and function of a Statutory Institution,

(b) two (2) shall be appointed by the Estates-General,

(c) one (1) shall be an Assistant Undersecretary assigned by the Undersecretary,

(d) one (1) shall be a chief executive of any other executive institution appointed by the Sovereign,

(e) one (1) shall be appointed by the private sector for every six percent (6%) in equity held by the private sector, and

(f) no more than three (3) shall be appointed by the Sovereign at his sole discretion, such that the final number of Trustees is an odd number.

(3) The Chairman of the Board of Trustees shall be appointed from the Trustees by the Secretary of State, who shall preside over the meetings of the Board of Trustees.

(4) The Chief Executive shall be the chief executive of a Statutory Corporation and shall report directly to its Board of Trustees.

(5) The Board of Trustees shall, on a quarterly basis and in the absence of any other period specified by its authorising Act of Estates:

(a) provide oversight consistent with the Act of Estates;

(b) supervise the performance of the Statutory Corporation;

(c) ensure proper governance, accountability, and internal controls;

(d) approve fiscal matters, including requests for budgets and major expenditures, sale and acquisition of assets, and debt issuance;

(e) approve policies and matters necessary for corporate governance;

(f) advise the Secretary of State on the appointment and removal of the Chief Executive; and

(g) perform such functions as are necessary for the proper governance of the Statutory Corporation.

(6) The Chief Executive shall in respect of his Statutory Corporation:

(a) be responsible for the day-to-day management;

(b) implement the directions of the Board of Trustees and the Sovereign;

(c) exercise executive authority over all officers and operations;

(d) ensure compliance with the Act of Estates and applicable law; and

(e) perform such functions as are necessary for effective administration.

(7) A Statutory Corporation shall not issue Regulations, save where expressly provided for in the originating Act of Estates, and shall be of limited scope and of discrete application.

(8) The Board of Trustees shall direct the Chief Executive to hold assets where necessary for revenue generation, as collateral for debt, or a combination of both and for any commercial purpose, such that its operations are effectively sustained without significant transfers from the Secretary of State. It shall be the responsibility of the Chief Executive to request for such transfers from the Secretary of State and to notify the Board of Trustees on the financial circumstances that would justify such transfers.

(9) The total debt issued by the Statutory Corporation, inclusive of the total interest to be paid over the debt servicing period, shall not exceed one hundred percent (100%) of the assets held by the Statutory Corporation or two hundred and fifty percent (250%) of the cumulative gross revenue of the past ten (10) years, whichever is higher. It shall be the responsibility of the Chairman of the Board to notify and seek the approval of the Secretary of State where the debt to be issued exceeds the ceiling.

(10) The Chief Executive, with the permission of the Board of Trustees, may sell no more than twenty-five percent (25%) of equity in the Statutory Corporation to any private sector entity, provided that the Estates-General ratifies the sale. A repurchase of the sold equity shall not require the ratification of the Estates-General.

(11) A private sector entity with interest in equity of a Statutory Corporation shall not be entitled to dividends within the first ten (10) years of acquiring that interest and thereafter shall be entitled to no more than ninety percent (90%) of any dividend that his interest in the equity would have been paid out as. Where the Statutory Corporation repurchases the equity from a private sector entity within the first ten (10) years of the private sector entity acquiring such an interest, the private sector entity is entitled to receive a value equivalent to his initial capital and a five percent (5%) annual interest for each year since the acquisition of such interest, or the current market value of the repurchased equity, whichever is greater.

(12) A private sector entity shall not transfer the ownership of equity in a Statutory Corporation without first notifying the Chairman of the Board of Trustees and seeking the approval of the Secretary of State.

(13) In the event of a bankruptcy or insolvency of a private sector entity with equity in a Statutory Corporation, the Secretary of State shall make all arrangements necessary for the repurchase of the equity held by the private sector entity.

17. Adjudicative and Appellative Powers

Where the Estates-General has not provided for appeals and adjudicative processes over the exercise of executive authority for an executive institution, or where such processes have been directed to the executive institution itself, the Secretary of State may direct that the executive institution enacts Regulations to clarify such processes by conferring to a judicial body or a specialised body within the executive institution such appellative or adjudicative powers, or in the absence or inadequacy of such Regulations, choose to hear such appeals himself or direct an Undersecretary or Assistant Undersecretary to hear such appeals.

18. Non-Exhaustive Powers of Secretary of State

The Estates-General may, from time to time, codify specific powers for a Secretary of State in an Act of Estates, which shall not be construed to limit the non-exhaustive scope of powers of the Secretary of State in accordance with the principles laid down in this Order of State, but merely a codified structuring on the exercise of such powers necessary for the consistent and orderly conduct of executive affairs by the State.
