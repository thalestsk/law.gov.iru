Cantonal Governance Order 2024

An Order of State to layout the legal framework for governance at the Cantonal level.
Reference No: OS.2024/01 Enacted: 1 January 2024

By Order of His Imperial Majesty Emperor Thales XIII, Sovereign of the State of Common Good

1. Short title, etc.

This Order of State is the Cantonal Governance Order 2024 as promulgated on 1 January 2024.

2. Administrative Divisions

(1) The Domain is to be subdivided into administrative units known as “Cantons”. The size, territorial limits and names of the Cantons shall be determined by the Sovereign.

(2) A Canton is further subdivided into administrative units known as “Prefectures”. The names of Prefectures shall be determined by the Cantonal Authorities in a manner that befits practicality, coherence, identity and culture.

(3) A Prefecture is further subdivided into administrative units known as “Municipalities”. The names of Municipalities shall be determined by the Cantonal Authorities in a manner that befits practicality, coherence, identity and culture.

(4) In the determination of the size and territorial limits of the Prefectures and Municipalities by the Cantonal Authority, due consideration shall be given to the population and demographics such that the material imbalances in representation and vote equality will not manifest from such subdivision.

(5) Any further subdivision shall be taken to be purely administrative and without legal effect.

3. Cantonal Authority

(1) The Cantonal Authority is the collective body of executive authority for local government in a Canton.

(2) Where the plural form “Cantonal Authorities” is used, it shall refer to a specified grouping of prior-named Cantonal Authorities or all the Cantonal Authorities where the context may require and not to mean the individual parts of the Cantonal Authority within any Canton.

(3) The terms “Canton” or “Cantonal Authority” are taken to be interchangeable unless otherwise defined or where the context requires.

(4) The Cantonal Authority has provenance of authority from its Intendant-General and authority is discharged through its First Minister in accordance with written law.

(5) The executive authority of the Cantonal Authority is restricted to matters of and pertaining to Devolved Powers.

(6) Any act or omission of the Cantonal Authority is governed by this Order of State, all Acts of Estates, and any Cantonal Statute effected by Parliament.

4. Parliament

(1) The collective body of legislative authority within a Canton shall be known as its “Parliament”.

(2) Where the plural form “Parliaments” is used, it shall refer to more than one or all of the Parliaments present in the Domain, where the context requires.

(3) The legislative authority of Parliament is restricted to matters of and pertaining to Devolved Powers.

(4) Parliament is to be organised unicamerally for the origination, deliberation, amendment and passage of laws and motions, comprising Members of Parliament (the “Member”) and chaired by a Speaker of Parliament (“the Speaker”).

(5) The manner of proceedings, standing orders and the business of Parliament shall be determined by Parliament alone.

(6) Unless otherwise provided for by Parliament:

(a) the quorum required for the conduct of business in Parliament is at least two-thirds of the total number of Members,

(b) the majority decision of the present Members, measured as a simple majority, shall be the taken as the decision of Parliament, and

(c) the Speaker shall refrain from participating in any vote in Parliament, except in the event where the vote cast by the Speaker may change the outcome of the vote that the Speaker shall vote to maintain the status quo ante.

(7) Each Municipality elects one (1) Member. Parliament may make express provisions to elect any number of additional members such that the final number of Members elected does not exceed two (2) Members for each Municipality in the Canton.

5. Devolved Powers

The Devolved Powers that fall within the purview of the Cantonal Authority and Parliament shall be limited to, unless otherwise expressly provided for in written law:

(a) education delivery, healthcare administration, social welfare, family and community services,

(b) housing, urban planning, land use and local infrastructure, physical and spatial organisation within the Canton,

(c) employment support, skills training, apprenticeships, labour market integration, workforce development

(d) local enterprise, investment promotion, tourism, agriculture, local economic strategy, business grants,

(e) environmental conservation, climate adaptation, parks and recreation, municipal services, resource stewardship,

(f) community safety, emergency rescue services, crime prevention and civil awareness, criminal prosecution,

(g) culture, heritage, arts, ceremonies, civic identity,

(h) civil registration, electoral administration and local self-government, and

(i) any other powers expressly provided for by an Act of Estates insofar as it complies with the principles of this Order of State.

6. Expenditure for Devolved Powers

(1) The Government may allocate funds to Cantonal Authorities for expenditure on matters on and pertaining to Devolved Powers.

(2) Parliament may raise additional revenue to fund the Cantonal Authority’s expenditure on matters on and pertaining to Devolved Powers.

(3) Parliament shall retain its right to determine, control, scrutinise, withhold and review the expenditure of the funds raised by or allocated to the Cantonal Authority as and when it may deem necessary for fiscal prudence, but shall not exercise such right to the effect of frustrating the functions of the Cantonal Authority.

(4) The Cantonal Authority shall periodically report the total amount received and expended by the Canton to the Intendant-General and Parliament.

7. Governing Principle of Devolved Powers

The Government shall determine the framework and minimum standards essential to the many aspects of public life. The Cantonal Authority and Parliament may go further to determine the implementation and adaptation of the framework and minimum standards to local conditions within the Canton but shall in no way undermine such framework and standards.

8. Intendant-General of State

The Intendant-General of State (the “Intendant-General”) of a Canton is the resident official of the Sovereign in that Canton and serves at the pleasure of the Sovereign.

9. Function of Intendant-General

The Intendant-General is responsible for:

(a) the appointment of the executive in his respective Cantonal Authority,

(b) the sufficiency of governance within the Canton, and

(c) any ceremonial duties prescribed in written law and delegated by the Sovereign.

10. Sufficiency of Governance

The dimensions of sufficiency shall be of the following characteristics:

(a) constitutional, on the limits of local governance;

(b) institutional, on the appointment of officeholders;

(c) governmental, on the practical capacity of the appointed officeholders; and

(d) democratic, on the electoral context applicable within the Canton.

11. Powers of the Intendant-General

(1) The Intendant-General may from time to time and at his sole discretion:

(a) appoint to or remove from service any person as the Prefect-General and Prefects,

(b) appoint no more than one (1) Member as Acting Minister for each vacancy in the office of a Minister and where the First Minister is unable to fill such vacancy, and

(c) veto any legislation passed by Parliament.

(2) The Intendant-General may at his sole discretion upon a formal request from his First Minister:

(a) appoint a Member to serve as a Minister reporting to the First Minister,

(b) prorogue Parliament, and

(c) dissolve Parliament.

(3) The Intendant-General shall at his sole discretion and with full expeditiousness:

(a) appoint the Member elected by Parliament as the First Minister,

(b) remove the First Minister from office where impeached by Parliament,

(c) appoint or remove any Member as Acting First Minister where Parliament has yet to elect a First Minister notwithstanding such vacancy, whether actual or anticipated, and

(d) appoint or remove any number of Members as Acting Ministers to assist any Acting First Minister.

12. Additional Powers of the Intendant-General

(1) Where an Act of Estates purports to confer any additional powers to the Intendant-General, such additional powers shall remain within the limit of sufficiency of governance in the Canton and not extend to the actual governance and the operations pertaining thereto.

(2) Save for any Act of Estates, no other written law shall purport to confer any additional powers to the Intendant-General.

13. First Minister

(1) The First Minister is the chief executive of the Cantonal Authority.

(2) The First Minister may request the Intendant-General to appoint a Member as a Minister, either to fill a vacancy in an office created by written law or as part of the leadership team of the Cantonal Authority as deemed necessary by the First Minister.

(3) A Minister may act with the full authority of the First Minister unless otherwise limited in scope or authority in any written law.

(4) Any act or omission of a Minister constitutes an act or omission of the First Minister and by extension constitutes an act or omission by the Cantonal Authority.

(5) The First Minister may overcome a veto on any legislation by the Intendant-General provided that the legislation was originated by the Cantonal Authority and was passed with a two-thirds majority. The First Minister may not act in any manner to pre-emptively frustrate the Intendant-General from exercising his veto.

(6) Unless otherwise expressly provided for in written law, the First Minister may determine the structure, composition and organisation of the Cantonal Authority for the effective governance of the Canton.

14. Prefect-General and Prefects

(1) Prefects-General and Prefects are public service officers employed by the Cantonal Authority to assist the First Minister in the administration of the Cantonal Authority and discharge of their executive authority in their daily business.

(2) The Prefect-General may appoint any number of deputies to assist him in his duties.

(3) The Prefect-General shall advise the Intendant-General on the appointment of Prefects.

(4) The Prefects shall appoint, with the concurrence of the Prefect-General, any number of deputies to assist them in their duties.

(5) The number of Prefects is determined by the number of Prefectures in a Canton.

(6) No Prefect-General or Prefect shall frustrate the lawful discharge of authority of the Cantonal Authority and shall remain politically neutral in the discharge of their duties.

15. Mayors

(1) Mayors are directly elected from the Prefectures and report to the First Minister or a Minister designated by the First Minister.

(2) The Mayor shall assist the First Minister in the discharge of executive authority within the Prefecture.

(3) The scope of executive authority of the Mayor rests solely on the municipal management of the Prefectures and any ceremonial duties prescribed in written law or deemed customary from time to time.

(4) Parliament may legislate on any derogation or expansion of the executive authority of the Mayor, save where such derogation serves to frustrate the functions of the Mayor or where such expansion removes ultimate responsibility from the First Minister.

16. Acting Capacity

An Acting First Minister or Acting Minister shall only act where necessary to preserve the status quo ante where Parliament has not elected a First Minister and for the safe and orderly conduct of elections where Parliament has been dissolved.

17. Extraordinary Powers

Where Parliament has expressly provided for the First Minister to exercise extraordinary powers with the legal effect to bypass the functions of Parliament expressed and implied in this Order of State, such powers may only be exercised with the concurrence of the Intendant-General or Parliament. Where Parliament has concurred, the First Minister shall notify the Intendant-General in writing of his intent prior to the exercise of such powers.

18. Elections and Terms of Office

(1) Members and Mayors shall be elected concurrently in the same election.

(2) The term of office for Members and Mayors shall commence on the first day of the month that occurs following the seven (7) days upon which the final vote cast was counted.

(3) The term of office for Members and Mayors shall continue after the dissolution of Parliament until the next term of office has begun.

(4) The Intendant-General shall dissolve Parliament no later than fifty (50) months after the commencement of the term of office for Members and Mayors.

19. Recall Petitions

Unless otherwise expressly provided for by Parliament, recall petitions shall not have any legal effect.

20. Vacancies during the Term of Office

Where a vacancy arises during the term of office, whether by death, incapacity, resignation, impeachment or any event specified in written law, the First Minister shall organise a special election to fill such a vacancy within one hundred (100) days of the vacancy.

21. Eligibility

A citizen residing in the Canton is eligible to be elected as a Member or Mayor, provided that:

(a) the citizen meets other eligibility criteria specified in written law, and

(b) where the citizen previously held elected office, the citizen completed his term of office, or

(c) where the citizen previously held elected office but had not completed his term of office, at least one (1) full term of office immediately following the incomplete term of office has elapsed.

22. Cantonal Statute

Any written law made by Parliament shall be known as a “Cantonal Statute” and its application is limited to within the jurisdiction of the Canton, insofar as it complies with other written laws of superseding hierarchy.

23. Regulations

(1) Where a Cantonal Statute confers a right to the Cantonal Authority to issue regulations, such a regulation is limited in its application to within the jurisdiction of the Canton, insofar as it complies with other written laws of superseding hierarchy.

(2) Where such Regulation may conflict with a Regulation made under an Act of Estates, the Regulation made under an Act of Estates shall take precedence.

(3) In the resolution of conflicts between Regulations made under Cantonal Statutes and Acts of Estates, the interpretation which produces the lowest superseding effect shall prevail, insofar as it continues to comply with other written laws of superseding hierarchy.
