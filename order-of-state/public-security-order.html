Public Security Order 2017

An Order of State for the establishment of the Directorate-General of Public Security.
Reference No: OS.2017/04 Enacted: 1 January 2017

By Order of His Imperial Majesty Emperor Thales XIII, Sovereign of the State of Common Good

1. Short title, etc.

This Order of State is the Public Security Order 2017 as promulgated on 1 January 2017.

2. Directorate-General of Public Security

The Directorate-General of Public Security (the "Directorate-General") is hereby established as the umbrella organisation for all public security functions.

3. Public Security Officer

(1) A public security officer is any person employed in the Directorate-General who is empowered to exercise public security functions.

(2) A public security officer shall be automatically deemed to be an authorised officer with respect to the enforcement against all manners of offences under any written law, be it administrative, criminal or regulatory, unless specifically excluded under written law.

(3) The Director-General may retrospectively designate any person as an acting public security officer if they are found to be acting in accordance with the functions and duties of a public security officer, provided that the situation would require the immediate intervention of a public security officer and where a public security officer is not immediately available to exercise such duties.

4. Leadership Structure

(1) The chief executive officer of the Directorate-General shall be a public security officer and appointed by the Sovereign.

(2) The Director-General may appoint other public security officers as Deputy Directors-General or Assistant Directors-General for the effective administration of the Directorate-General.

(3) The Deputy Directors-General and Assistant Directors-General may appoint other public security officers as needed for the effective exercise of their respective functions and duties as determined by the Director-General.

(4) The Director-General may make standing instructions on the line of succession within the Directorate-General in the event of the incapacity or absence of the any member of the leadership, unless otherwise provided for by the Sovereign.

5. Public Security Functions

Public security officers are empowered to exercise powers necessary for:

(a) upholding public order;

(b) prevention, investigation and deterrence of crime;

(c) protection of life and property;

(d) enforcement of law; and

(e) maintenance of trust in the public security apparatus.

6. Estates-General

The Estates-General shall provide for the procedures, protocols and standards for the exercise of public security functions, such that they are consistent with the principles of the Order of State, without encumbering the effectiveness of the public security apparatus.

7. Specified Functions

The Director-General shall make provisions to organise within the Directorate-General on the following specified functions:

(a) policing and public safety;

(b) border control;

(c) corrections;

(d) crime investigation; and

(e) any other function accorded under written law or deemed necessary by the Director-General for the effective exercise of public security functions.

8. Absence of Written Law

In the absence of written law or legal provisions containing or prescribing the structured exercise of powers in the discharge of public security functions, the Director-General may direct the use of powers necessary for the effective exercise such functions, and neither the Director-General or a public security officer directed as such shall not be liable for the use of such powers unless otherwise provided by law.

9. Direct Responsibility to Sovereign

The Director-General bears sole, direct and personal responsibility to the Sovereign until otherwise discharged by the Sovereign.
