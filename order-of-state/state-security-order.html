State Security Order 2017

An Order of State for the establishment of the Directorate-General of State Security.
Reference No: OS.2017/03 Enacted: 1 January 2017

By Order of His Imperial Majesty Emperor Thales XIII, Sovereign of the State of Common Good

1. Short title, etc.

This Order of State is the State Security Order 2017 as promulgated on 1 January 2017.

2. Directorate-General of State Security

The Directorate-General of State Security (the "Directorate-General") is hereby established as the umbrella organisation for all state security functions.

3. State Security Officer

(1) A state security officer is any person employed in the Directorate-General who is empowered to exercise state security functions.

(2) A state security officer may exercise the powers of a public security officer in support of public security functions under Public Security Order 2017 (OS.2017/04) where the situation requires but shall not be compelled to do so or automatically deemed to have assumed the responsibility of a public security officer.

4. Leadership Structure

(1) The chief executive officer of the Directorate-General shall be a state security officer and appointed by the Sovereign.

(2) The Director-General may appoint other public security officers as Deputy Directors-General or Assistant Directors-General for the effective administration of the Directorate-General.

(3) The Deputy Directors-General and Assistant Directors-General may appoint other public security officers as needed for the effective exercise of their respective functions and duties as determined by the Director-General.

(4) The Director-General may make standing instructions on the line of succession within the Directorate-General in the event of the incapacity or absence of the any member of the leadership, unless otherwise provided for by the Sovereign.

5. State Security Functions

State security officers are empowered to exercise powers necessary for:

(a) protection of the sovereignty and territorial integrity of the State;

(b) prevention, investigation and deterrence of threats to national security;

(c) intervention against subversion and espionage;

(d) furtherance of national interests at the international level;

(e) maintenance of trust in the state security apparatus.

6. Estates-General

The Estates-General shall provide for the procedures, protocols and standards for the exercise of state security functions, such that they are consistent with the principles of the Order of State, without encumbering the effectiveness of the state security apparatus.

7. Organisation

The Director-General shall make provisions to organise within the Directorate-General into departments and divisions as deemed necessary for the effective exercise of state security functions.

8. Absence of Written Law

In the absence of written law or legal provisions containing or prescribing the structured exercise of powers in the discharge of state security functions, the Director-General may direct the use of powers necessary for the effective exercise such functions, and neither the Director-General or a state security officer directed as such shall not be liable for the use of such powers unless otherwise provided by law.

9. Direct Responsibility to Sovereign

The Director-General bears sole, direct and personal responsibility to the Sovereign until otherwise discharged by the Sovereign.
