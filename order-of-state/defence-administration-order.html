Defence Administration Order 2017

An Order of State for the establishment of the National Defence Administration.
Reference No: OS.2017/02 Enacted: 1 January 2017

By Order of His Imperial Majesty Emperor Thales XIII, Sovereign of the State of Common Good

1. Short title, etc.

This Order of State is the National Defence Administration Order 2017 as promulgated on 1 January 2017.

2. National Defence Administration

The National Defence Administration (the "Administration") is hereby established as the umbrella organisation for all functions pertaining to the administration of national defence.

3. Leadership Structure

(1) The chief executive officer of the Administration shall be the Director-General of Defence Administration ("Director-General") and shall be appointed by the Sovereign.

(2) The Director-General may appoint other persons as Deputy Directors-General or Assistant Directors-General for the effective administration of the Administration.

(3) The Deputy Directors-General and Assistant Directors-General may appoint other persons as needed for the effective exercise of their respective functions and duties as determined by the Director-General.

(4) The Director-General may make standing instructions on the line of succession within the Administration in the event of the incapacity or absence of the any member of the leadership, unless otherwise provided for by the Sovereign.

4. Scope of Administrative Authority

The Director-General has authority over:

(a) fiscal and financial matters essential to the effective administration and operation of national defence;

(b) the participation, care, maintenance and welfare of all persons employed for the purposes of national defence, be it in the past, present or future;

(c) building and maintaining current and future capabilities for the purposes of national defence;

(d) the effective implementation and facilitiation of transition of the national economy to support and sustain a prolonged state of total war; and

(e) formulating, executing and supporting of national defence outcomes that may become necessary from time to time.

5. Estates-General

The Estates-General may not directly provide for matters of national defence, and where a provision on a matter of principle or general governance may be applicable, such a provision shall not be constructed or construed to encumber the operational effectiveness for the delivery of national defence outcomes.

6. Organisation

The Director-General shall make provisions to organise within the Administration into departments and divisions as deemed necessary for the effective administration of national defence.

7. Direct Responsibility to Sovereign

The Director-General bears sole, direct and personal responsibility to the Sovereign until otherwise discharged by the Sovereign.
