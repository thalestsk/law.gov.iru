Judiciary Order 2017

An Order of State for the structuring of judicial system.
Reference No: OS.2017/05 Enacted: 1 January 2017

By Order of His Imperial Majesty Emperor Thales XIII, Sovereign of the State of Common Good

1. Short title, etc.

This Order of State is the Judiciary Order 2017 as promulgated on 1 January 2017.

2. Judicial Officer

A judicial officer is a person who has been admitted to the bar for no less than five (5) years and is employed by the judiciary to exercise judicial functions.

3. Divisional Head

(1) A Divisional Head is the chief executive officer of the court where he is a judicial officer and has assumed the responsibility to exercise the corresponding judicial functions.

(2) The style of the Divisional Head signifies his position as primus inter pares and shall not be construed as having power or authority over the judicial functions of the other judicial officers.

(3) There shall be one (1) Divisional Head for each instance of division of a court.

4. Structure of the Judiciary

The judiciary is composed of three (3) judicial bodies, namely:

(a) the Supreme Court of Judicature (the “Court”),

(b) the High Court of the State (the “High Court”), and

(c) the Subordinate General Courts of the High Court of the State (the “Subordinate Courts”).

5. Divisions of the Court

The Court shall conduct business within its three (3) divisions, namely:

(a) the Grand Justiciary,

(b) the Court of Appeal, and

(c) the Court of Cassation.

6. Divisions of the High Court

The High Court shall conduct business within its three (3) divisions, namely:

(a) the Originating Division,

(b) the Appellate Division, and

(c) the Review Division.

7. Divisions of the Subordinate Courts

The Court shall conduct business within the following divisions:

(a) the Summary Magistracy,

(b) the Summons Magistracy,

(c) the Small Claims Magistracy, and

(d) other courts established by written law.

8. Summary Magistracy

(1) The Summary Magistracy has jurisdiction over:

(a) all criminal offences where the punishment does not exceed a fine of €15,000 and/or six (6) months’ imprisonment; and

(b) all regulatory and administrative offences where the punishment does not exceed a fine of €30,000.

(2) All proceedings in the Summary Magistracy shall be summary trials.

(3) All proceedings hereunder shall be presided over by a Magistrate.

(4) The Divisional Head of a Summary Magistracy shall be a Magistrate and shall be styled as “Chief Magistrate”.

9. Summons Magistracy

(1) The Summons Magistracy has jurisdiction over:

(a) all criminal offences where the punishment does not exceed a fine of €50,000 and/or seven (7) years’ imprisonment,

(b) all regulatory and administrative offences beyond the purview of the Summary Magistracy,

(c) all civil claims where the total amount in damages does not exceed €150,000.

(2) All proceedings hereunder shall be presided over by a Magistrate.

(3) The Divisional Head of a Summons Magistracy shall be a Magistrate and shall be styled as “Chief Magistrate”.

10. Small Claims Magistracy

(1) The Small Claims Magistracy has jurisdiction over all civil claims where:

(a) the total amount in damages does not exceed €30,000, or

(b) the parties agree in writing to file the claim at the Small Claims Magistracy for claims up to €50,000; and

(c) the contract relates to a sale of goods or provision of services,

(d) the contract relates to a lease of property not exceeding 2 years, or

(e) the dispute relates to a tort in respect to damage to property.

(2) All proceedings hereunder shall be presided over by a Magistrate.

(3) The Divisional Head of a Small Claims Magistracy shall be a Magistrate from the Summons Magistracy and shall be styled as “Chief Magistrate”.

11. Other Courts of the Subordinate Courts

(1) The procedure and manner of proceedings in the other courts of the Subordinate Courts shall be determined in the same way the courts were established.

(2) All proceedings hereunder shall be presided over by a Magistrate.

(3) The Divisional Head of such courts shall be a sitting Magistrate, and where such courts are not presided over by a Magistrate, shall be a Magistrate from the Summons Magistracy, and shall be styled as “Chief Magistrate”.

12. Originating Division of the High Court

(1) The Originating Division has jurisdiction over:

(a) criminal offences and civil claims exceeding the jurisdiction of the Summons Magistracy, and

(b) criminal offences and civil claims not belonging to the sole jurisdiction of the other courts of Subordinate Courts.

(2) All proceedings hereunder shall be presided over by a Judge or Judicial Commissioner.

(3) A Judge in the Originating Division may become the Divisional Head and shall be styled as “Justice of the High Court”.

13. Appellate Division of the High Court

(1) The Appellate Division has jurisdiction over appeals of decisions of the Subordinate Courts.

(2) All proceedings hereunder shall be presided over by a Judge.

(3) A Judge in the Appellate Division may become the Divisional Head and shall be styled as “Justice of the High Court”.

14. Review Division of the High Court

(1) The Review Division has jurisdiction over reviews pertaining to:

(a) matters of procedure provided for in Regulations, and

(b) matters of law and procedure on the exercise of executive and legislative powers by a Cantonal Authority or Parliament.

(2) All proceedings hereunder shall be presided over by a Judge.

(3) A Judge in the Review Division may become the Divisional Head and shall be styled as “Justice of the High Court”.

15. Court of Appeal

(1) The Court of Appeal has jurisdiction over appeals of decisions of:

(a) the Originating Division, and

(b) the Appellate Division with leave.

(2) All proceedings hereunder shall be presided over by a Justice of Appeal.

(3) A Justice of Appeal in the Court of Appeal may become the Divisional Head and shall be styled as “Associate Justice”.

(4) A decision of the Court of Appeal shall not be appealed but may be reviewed by the Grand Justiciary.

16. Court of Cassation

(1) The Court of Cassation has:

(a) first-instance jurisdiction over reviews pertaining to matters of procedure and law on the exercise of executive and legislative power by the Government, and

(b) appellate jurisdiction over decisions of the Review Division on matters of law.

(2) A Justice of Appeal in the Court of Appeal may become the Divisional Head and shall be styled as “Associate Justice”.

(3) Where the Court of Cassation is exercising appellate jurisdiction, the Divisional Head and two (2) other Justices of Appeal of the Court of Cassation shall preside over the proceedings and any decision made therein shall be a unanimous decision of the presiding judges.

(4) A decision of the Court of Cassation shall not be appealed but may be reviewed by the Grand Justiciary, save for where the Court of Cassation has exercised first-instance jurisdiction.

17. Grand Justiciary

(1) The Grand Justiciary has:

(a) review jurisdiction over all decisions within the Court,

(b) review jurisdiction over all decisions of the High Court, and

(c) appellate jurisdiction over a decision of the Court of Cassation where the Court of Cassation has exercised first-instance jurisdiction.

(2) The Grand Justiciary comprises seven (7) Chief Justices.

(3) The Chief Justices shall jointly and severally undertake the role of Divisional Head of the Grand Justiciary.

(4) All proceedings of the Grand Justiciary shall be presided over by all the Chief Justices and any decision made therein shall not be dissented by more than two (2) Chief Justices.

(5) Only the Sovereign, acting upon the advice of the Chief Justices but at his sole discretion, may provide for matters pertaining to procedure and organisation of the Grand Justiciary.

18. Judiciary Standing Committee

(1) The Judiciary Standing Committee (the “Committee”) shall be the supervisory committee of the entire judicial system.

(2) The Committee shall provide for the administration and organisation for the effective operation of the courts, and for the remuneration, privileges and welfare of judicial officers.

(3) The Committee shall form sub-committees, ad-hoc or standing, to provide for matters relating to:

(a) the nomination of judicial officers for appointment by the Sovereign,

(b) disciplinary hearings and the code of conduct for judicial officers,

(c) the appointment of non-judicial officers to the Committee,

(d) the organisation and conduct of public outreach events, seminars and initiatives,

(e) the appointment of the administrative leadership to assist Divisional Heads in the administration of the courts, and

(f) other matters that may affect public trust in the judiciary.

(4) The Committee shall comprise:

(a) all Divisional Heads,

(b) no more than ten (10) members appointed by the Attorney-General inclusive of the Attorney-General himself,

(c) Counsels of State appointed by the Sovereign, and

(d) other non-judicial officers appointed by a sub-committee of the Committee.

19. Designations of "Deputy" and "Senior"

(1) Where a judicial officer is styled with the terms "Deputy" or "Senior" as a prefix to their title, the terms may be used to denote seniority, order of precedence between the judicial officers of the same rank, and administrative purposes such as remuneration.

(2) The term "Deputy" shall be assigned to all judicial officers in the High Court and Subordinate Courts who have held their current rank for no more than five (5) years.

(3) The term "Senior" shall be assigned to all judicial officers in the High Court and Subordinate Courts who have held their current rank for more than thirteen (13) years.

20. Court Orders

(1) With respect to any matter brought before the courts, the presiding judge may serve:

(a) on a party to the proceeding, a writ, as an official order to comply with an act or omission,

(b) on a person, a subpoena, as an official order to be present before the court, and

(c) on a defendant in a criminal matter, a warrant, as an authorisation for his arrest and the search and seizure of his property by a public security officer.

(2) Only a higher court or the same court may revoke or amend an order of the courts.

21. Judicial Commissioner

The First Minister of a Canton may appoint no more than five (5) persons who are qualified to be judicial officers to serve as Judicial Commissioners to the High Court in that Canton.

22. Court Registry

(1) Each instance of division shall have its own registry, headed by the Registrar with the assistance of Assistant Registrars.

(2) A Registrar shall be appointed by the Committee.

(3) The Registrar of a court may appoint Assistant Registrars with the concurrence of the Divisional Head.

(4) A Registrar or Assistant Registrar shall have the authority over case administration, court fees, submission and issuance of documents and court orders, and other administrative duties as prescribed by the Committee for the effective disposal of business of the courts.

23. Master of the Court

(1) Each instance of division of the High Court and Subordinate Courts shall have its own Master of the Court (the "Master"), who shall be responsible for the administration and management of court business not undertaken by the Registrar or Assistant Registrars, such as the clerical staff, court security and facilities management.

(2) Masters of the High Court and the Subordinate Courts shall be nominated by the First Minister and appointed by the Divisional Head.

24. Chancellor of the Court

There shall be a Chancellor of the Court (the "Chancellor") to fulfil the duties of the Master for the Court.

The Chancellor shall be appointed by the Committee.

25. Order of Precedence

The order of precedence shall be as follows, in descending order:

(a) Chief Justice,

(b) Associate Justice,

(c) Justice of Appeals,

(d) Justice of the High Court,

(e) Judicial Commissioner,

(f) Judge, and

(g) Magistrate.

26. Honorifics

(1) The honorific title of "The Honourable" shall be used when addressing judicial officers of the High Court and Subordinate Courts, and "The Right Honourable" for judicial officers of the Court.

(2) All judicial officers shall be directly addressed as "Your Honour".
