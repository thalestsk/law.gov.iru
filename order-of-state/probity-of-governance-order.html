Probity of Governance Order 2024

An Order of State for the establishment of the Directorate-General of Supervision.
Reference No: OS.2024/03 Enacted: 1 January 2024

By Order of His Imperial Majesty Emperor Thales XIII, Sovereign of the State of Common Good

1. Short title, etc.

This Order of State is the Probity of Governance Order 2024 as promulgated on 1 January 2024.

2. Directorate-General of Supervision

The Directorate-General of Supervision (the "Directorate-General") is hereby established as the umbrella organisation for the supervision of the conduct of government.

3. Leadership Structure

(1) The Director-General of Supervision (the "Director-General") shall be the chief executive officer of the Directorate-General and shall be appointed by the Sovereign.

(2) The Director-General shall appoint three (3) Deputy Directors-General, who shall concurrently be appointed as:

(a) Inspector-General,

(b) Procurator-General, and

(c) Censor-General.

(3) The Deputy Directors-General shall appoint with the concurrence of the Director-General deputies and staff for the effective discharge of their duties.

(4) The Director-General may make standing instructions on the line of succession within the Directorate-General in the event of the incapacity or absence of a member of the leadership, unless otherwise provided for by the Sovereign.

4. Inspector-General

The Inspector-General shall be conferred the authority to:

(a) investigate allegations of corruption within the State;

(b) enact regulations on matters pertaining to enforcement operations against corruption;

(c) exercise the powers of public security officers in carrying out investigations and enforcement operations; and

(d) refer its investigations to the appropriate prosecuting authority for prosecution.

5. Procurator-General

The Procurator-General shall be conferred the authority to:

(a) investigate allegations of professional misconduct and administrative overreach by employees of the State;

(b) enact regulations on matters pertaining its investigatory functions;

(c) exercise the powers of public security officers in carrying out investigations; and

(d) refer its investigations to the appropriate prosecuting authority for prosecution.

6. Censor-General

The Censor-General shall be conferred the authority to:

(a) educate the Government on the principles of probity and governance;

(b) implement change management initiatives to promote probity and governance;

(c) communicate and build mutual trust with the public regarding the state of governance; and

(d) conduct regular supervisory audits on the Government to identify areas of improvement, and where necessary, stipulate corrective actions or refer potential violations to the Inspector-General or Procurator-General for investigation.

7. Estates-General

The Estates-General shall provide for the penalties and legal consequences of violations of principles of probity and governance consistent with this Order of State.

8. Frustration of Government

The Director-General shall ensure that in the exercise of authority against the Government, the work of the Directorate-General is not conducted in a way that frustrates the effective functioning of the Government, and where such a potential conflict may arise, the Director-General shall consult the Sovereign for direction on the appropriate course of action.

9. Direct Responsibility to Sovereign

The Director-General bears sole, direct and personal responsibility to the Sovereign until otherwise discharged by the Sovereign.
